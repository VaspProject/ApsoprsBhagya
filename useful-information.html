Useful Information for APSOPRS 2026 Attendees

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience during your visit to Guwahati for the APSOPRS 2026 conference, we have compiled essential information about the local climate, currency, payment options, electricity, and emergency contacts. This guide will help you prepare for your stay at the Mayfair Spring Valley Resort and navigate the city with ease.

Climate & Weather

Guwahati enjoys pleasantly warm and comfortable weather in November, ideal for the APSOPRS 2026 conference. Average temperatures range from a high of 27°C (81°F) to a low of 16°C (61°F), with mild days and cool nights. Light clothing is recommended for daytime, along with a light jacket or sweater for evenings.

Indian Currency

Indian rupee is the currency of India. The rupee's currency code is INR.

Credit Card

Credit cards are commonly accepted across India, especially in major urban centers and popular tourist spots such as hotels, department stores, shopping malls, and restaurants. While major brands like Visa, MasterCard, and American Express are widely usable, it is advisable to confirm acceptance with individual merchants in advance. For transactions at smaller businesses or in rural areas, it is prudent to carry alternative payment options like debit cards or cash. Additionally, notifying your bank of your travel plans can help prevent any transaction issues.

Money Exchange

In India, you can exchange your foreign currency into Indian Rupees (INR) at banks, authorized currency exchange centers, or foreign exchange counters. Before exchanging, check if old or damaged currency notes are accepted, and verify the exchange rates and fees. Ensure you have valid travel documents and identification, and consider exchanging at authorized centers to avoid issues or scams.

Currency Converter

For real-time currency conversion, you can use online tools like the one provided by XE. Visit XE Currency Converter to check the latest exchange rates for INR and plan your budget accordingly.

Electricity and Voltage

In India, the standard voltage is 230 volts at 50 Hertz, and outlets typically have three round pins or a combination of round and flat pins. If your devices aren't compatible, you can purchase a travel adapter at airports, electronics stores, or online, or ask your hotel's front desk if they have adapters available for guests.
